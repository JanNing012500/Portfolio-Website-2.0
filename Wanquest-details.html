Summary

WanQuest is a turn-based RPG featuring three player characters - a Mage, Thief, and Warrior - who fight as a team against a golem enemy. This game was created using Unity and incorporates many classic RPG mechanics, such as a unique set of moves that players can choose from on their turn for each of the 3 characters. The player also has access to individual inventories with items that can be used in combat. The enemy also has its own set of unique moves to utilize. At the start of each turn, players select either one skill or item by clicking on it during their turn for each character, and the golem randomly chooses one skill to use. However, rather than immediately carrying out the chosen skills, the game calculates the order in which the skills will be executed based on the speed value of each skill. The battle ends when either all 3 characters die or if the Golem's health pool is reduced to zero. The speed mechanic in WanQuest adds an element of risk to combat, as the player does not know the exact speed of the enemy's moves. This means that the player must take into account the potential consequences of choosing slower, higher damage moves versus the alternative of using quicker, lower damage moves. Spamming quick attacks may not be effective, as the golem can sustain these low-damage attacks and eventually defeat the players but it can ensure that their abilities will go through before the golemâ€™s abilities. On the other hand, risking slower high damage moves also carries the risk of the player taking more damage or not doing any damage at all due to a few moves the enemy has which can reflect or block attacks. This creates a gameplay loop in which the player must balance the risks of using slower abilities with the efficiency of using faster abilities and items.
