Introduction

SoundStream aims to enhance the user experience of browsing and purchasing wireless headphones. Inspired by the challenges faced by users on Sony's website, this project addresses common issues and utilizes a harmonized color palette, simplified layout, and emphasizes essential information to streamline the shopping experience.

Role

Solo project to learn the UX design process in Figma. With a rudimentary approach, I designed the UI/UX, researched user experiences and finalized design choices.

Problem Statements and Solution




Goals and Objectives

The ultimate goal of this project is to enable users to quickly and easily find the information they need and make informed decisions when selecting their preferred wireless headphones. Through the use of a harmonized blue/purple/magenta color palette, a simplified layout, and a focus on essential information, this design seeks to create a visually engaging and efficient shopping experience.

Design Direction

During my research of similar brands' websites for headphones, I found that important information was often difficult to find, and the use of a plain white color theme resulting in an unmemorable and generic shopping experience. To address these issues, I decided to implement a more vibrant color palette and include animations that help the most important aspects of each page stand out.
The overall design direction of this project was to create a visually engaging and user-friendly shopping experience that prioritizes essential information.

Challenges and Constraints

Throughout this project, I faced various challenges as this was my first time using Figma to design a UI/UX project. To overcome these challenges, I conducted extensive research and completed online courses to ensure that I could effectively utilize Figma to its full potential. Despite the learning curve, I was able to create a visually appealing design that helped solve the issues identified in the research phase.

In addition to researching and learning how to use Figma effectively, I also experimented with various design styles and created numerous assets and animations to find the ones that worked best for this project. This process allowed me to refine the design and ensure that it was not only visually appealing but also functional and intuitive for the user.


Conclusion

After conducting user research on Sony's website for wireless headphones, I identified several issues, including difficulty in finding warranty information, distracting model numbers in product titles, and an unoptimized review section. To address these challenges, I designed a colorful site with blur and drop shadow effects to highlight important elements to enhance the customer experience. Despite some challenges due to my limited experience with Figma, the final design improves the user's shopping experience by making essential information easier to find and increasing confidence in purchase decisions. The final product achieves the goal of enhancing the overall user experience by streamlining checkout and improving brand consistency through fun graphics, color palette, and font choice.
