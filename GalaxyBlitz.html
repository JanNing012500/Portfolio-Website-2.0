Galaxy Blitz

 Collect powerups, max stats, and enter Blitz!
Solo Project


Galaxy Blitz is an arcade-style shooter game featuring a side-to-side movement controlled by a single button that requires players to think and react quickly in order to achieve a high score. Players controls the direction of their character by clicking the mouse button or hold it down to stay in one spot.
I wanted to add a progression system to give the game more depth so as players progress through the game and collect power-ups, their bullet size and rate of fire both increase. Once players collect enough power-ups, they enter Blitz Time, which gives them a score multiplier and an extra life. Blitz time ends when the player loses a life. After initial playtesting, I added a full-screen map clear mechanic that triggers when players lose a life to give players a few split seconds to reposition themselves, as the speed of the game progressively increases.


Orbital Disco

Reach your dance partner!
Team Size : 6
Contributions: Movement mechanics, Sprite animations, Sfx


Orbital Disco challenges players to maneuver around the other dancer to reach their dance partner on the other side of the dancefloor. This game features a charged press and hold mechanic that determines how far the player dances. Direction is based on the position of the clockwise-orbiting indicator circling the player. With these two factors combined, players can precisely control both the distance and direction of their character using a single button. Other dancers are procedurally spawned and increase in number after each level.


Humanity's Demise

Destroy the Humans!
Team Size : 2
Contributions: Gameplay Programming


Humanity's Demise is another arcade-style shooter game where players aim to destroy mankind. As the game progresses, both the player and the enemy become stronger. After each wave, both the player and enemies gain more Health Points, faster shooting speed but the player has one advantage: more missiles which deal significant damage! Upon losing a life, players will send out a wave of bullets, but as the enemies become stronger after each round, these waves will no longer be as effective.
