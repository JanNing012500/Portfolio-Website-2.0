Gameplay Features

In Raveball Arena, players can dribble the ball by colliding with it and use a shooting bar mechanism to pass and shoot. The game features procedural level interactions, including musical events that cause the arena to shapeshift, spawn items, and change layouts or gamemodes. Players can also pick up items that spawn randomly, affecting their abilities, and interact with the ball and other players using their gun.

Since this is an event-based game, the music in Raveball Arena plays a major role in the gameplay loop, with the arena and other aspects of the game changing based on the music's energy and different phases, such as the beginning, buildup, drop, and breakdown. There are clear indications in the music when events are going to happen, and these events can have a major impact on the gameplay, such as walls rising to split from the ground. Passes and shots in the game are determined by a power bar where timing determines the launch speed. There are also random events, powerups, and obstacles that help keep the game exciting. The team that scores the most goals in a given round wins.

Role details

As a member of the Raveball Arena development team, I was responsible for implementing and researching various systems, including the dynamic crosshair UI, movement and animation systems, and Lyra UI systems and game modes. I also migrated into and exported from Lyra, and debugged various features such as player menu stuck bugs, health bar issues, and ball glitches. In addition, I designed the main menu of the game, adding gameplay options and both visual and sound effects for all the menu buttons. I also created blendspaces for animations and imported them to the character model. In order to allow players to have the option of playing multiplayer mode with their friends, I implemented a heads-up display for both 2 player and 4 player modes, including the scoreboard, event panels, and skills, and also worked on the local multiplayer system and dynamic ability cooldown. Several playtests were also conducted by me and we implemented changes based on feedback received during these sessions.
